## The island

Inis Oírr (Inisheer) is the smallest and easternmost of the three Aran
Islands, at the mouth of Galway Bay, County Galway, Ireland. It is a
Gaeltacht community — Irish is the everyday language — with a landscape of
limestone pavement, dry-stone walls and small green fields, closely related
to the Burren across the sound in County Clare.

## Getting here

- Passenger ferries run from Ros a' Mhíl (Rossaveal, Co. Galway) and
  seasonally from Doolin (Co. Clare).
- Aer Arann Islands operates short flights from Connemara Regional Airport
  (Indreabhán).
- Crossing/flight times vary by season and weather — check operators for
  current timetables.

## Practical basics

- The island is small (about 3 km across) — most places are walkable, and
  bikes are a popular way to get around.
- Landmarks include the Plassey shipwreck (of Father Ted title-sequence
  fame), O'Brien's Castle (Caisleán Uí Bhriain), the lighthouse, and Tobar
  Éinne holy well.
- The island has accommodation, food, crafts and pubs — use the live
  listings for what's open and when.
