## The island

Árainn Mhór (Arranmore) is the largest inhabited island off County Donegal,
Ireland, lying about 5 km off the coast from Burtonport (Ailt an Chorráin).
It is part of the Donegal Gaeltacht — Irish is spoken daily alongside
English, and the island has a strong tradition of music, storytelling and
sea-faring.

## Getting here

- Ferries run year-round between Burtonport and Leabgarrow (An Leadhb
  Gharbh), the island's main pier — the crossing takes around 15–20 minutes.
- Two ferry operators serve the island; sailing times vary by season.
  (Ask the agent for today's sailings via the ferry service, or check the
  operators' websites for timetables.)

## Practical basics

- The island is roughly 9 km long — walkable and very cyclable.
- There is a lighthouse at the north-west tip (Rinrawros Point).
- The island has shops, pubs, accommodation and eateries — use the live
  listings for what's open and when.
